Ursus arctos (Linnaeus, 1758) – brown bear

  • Morphological characteristics:

    The normal range of physical dimensions for a brown bear is a head-and-body length of 1.4 to 2.8 m (4 ft 7 in to 9 ft 2 in) and a shoulder height of 70 to 153 cm (2 ft 4 in to 5 ft 0 in). Average weight of 180 kg (400 lb) in males and 135 kg (298 lb) in females.

    Russia, Central Asia, China, Canada, the United States, Scandinavia and the Carpathian region, especially Romania), Anatolia, and Caucasus

Ursus americanus(Pallas, 1780) – American black bear

  • Morphological characteristics:

    Adults typically range from 120 to 200 cm (47 to 79 in) in head-and-body length, and 70 to 105 cm (28 to 41 in) in shoulder height. Adult males typically weigh between 57–250 kg (126–551 lb), while females weigh 33% less at 41–170 kg (90–375 lb).

  • Distribution:

    The current range of black bears in the United States is constant throughout most of the Northeast, and within the Appalachian Mountains almost continuously from Maine to north Georgia, the northern Midwest, the Rocky Mountain region, the West Coast and Alaska.

Ursus thibetanus(G. Cuvier, 1823) – Asiatic black bear

  • Morphological characteristics:

    Adults measure 70–100 cm (28–39 in) at the shoulder, and 120–190 cm (47–75 in) in length. Adult males weigh 60–200 kg (130–440 lb) with an average weight of about 135 kg (298 lb). Adult females weigh 40–125 kg (88–276 lb), and large ones up to 140 kg (310 lb).

  • Distribution:

    It lives in the Himalayas, in the northern parts of the Indian subcontinent, Korea, northeastern China, the Russian Far East, the Honshū and Shikoku islands of Japan, and Taiwan.

  • Conservation status:

  • Subspecies:

    • Ursus thibetanus formosanus- Formosan black bear
    • Ursus thibetanus gedrosianus- Baluchistan black bear
    • Ursus thibetanus japonicus- Japanese black bear
    • Ursus thibetanus laniger- Himalayan black bear
    • Ursus thibetanus mupinensis- Indochinese black bear
    • Ursus thibetanus thibetanus- Tibetan black bear
    • Ursus thibetanus ussuricus- Ussuri black bear

Ursus maritimus(Phipps, 1774) – polar bear

  • Morphological characteristics:

    Adult male polar bears weigh 350–700 kg (772–1,543 lb) and measure 2.4–3 metres (7 ft 10 in–9 ft 10 in) in total length.

  • Distribution:

    Arctic Circle and adjacent land masses as far south as Newfoundland

  • Conservation status:

  • Subspecies:

    • Ursus maritimus maritimus
    • Ursus maritimus marinus

Helarctos malayanus(Raffles, 1821) – sun bear

  • Morphological characteristics:

    The sun bear is the smallest of the bear species. Adults are about 120–150 cm (47–59 in) long and weigh 27–80 kg (60–176 lb). Males are 10–20% larger than females.

  • Distribution:

    Sun bears are found in the tropical rainforest of Southeast Asia ranging from northeastern India, Bangladesh, Myanmar, Thailand, Peninsular Malaysia, Laos, Cambodia, Vietnam to southern Yunnan Province in China, and on the islands of Sumatra and Borneo in Indonesia.

  • Conservation status:

  • Subspecies:

    • Helarctos malayanus malayanus
    • Helarctos malayanus euryspilus

Melursus ursinus(Shaw, 1791) – sloth bear

  • Morphological characteristics:

    Sloth bears adults are a medium-sized species though weight can range variously from 55 to 105 kg (121 to 231 lb) in typically-sized females and from 80 to 145 kg (176 to 320 lb) in typically-sized males. They are 60–92 cm (2 ft 0 in–3 ft 0 in) high at the shoulder, and have a body length of 1.4–1.9 m (4 ft 7 in–6 ft 3 in).

  • Distribution:

    The sloth bear's global range includes India, the southern lowlands of Nepal, and Sri Lanka. It is regionally extinct in Bangladesh.

  • Conservation status:

  • Subspecies:

    • Melursus ursinus ursinus- Indian sloth bear
    • Melursus ursinus inornatus- Sri Lankan sloth bear

Trspanarctos ornatus(Cuvier, 1825) – spectacled bear

  • Morphological characteristics:

    Males are a third larger than females in dimensions and sometimes twice their weight. Males can weigh from 100 to 200 kg (220 to 440 lb), and females can weigh from 35 to 82 kg (77 to 181 lb).[8] Head-and-body length can range from 120 to 200 cm (47 to 78.5 in), though mature males do not measure less than 150 cm (59 in).On average males weigh about 115 kg (254 lb) and females average about 65 kg (143 lb), thus it rivals the polar bear for the most sexually dimorphic modern bear.

  • Distribution:

    Despite some rare spilling-over into eastern Panama, spectacled bears are mostly restricted to certain areas of northern and western South America. They can range in western Venezuela,[18] Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, western Bolivia, and northwestern Argentina. The species is found almost entirely in the Andes Mountains.

  • Conservation status:

  • Subspecies:

    • none

Ailuropoda melanoleuca(David, 1869) – giant panda

  • Morphological characteristics:

    Adults measure around 1.2 to 1.9 m (4 to 6 ft) long, including a tail of about 10–15 cm (3.9–5.9 in), and 60 to 90 cm (2.0 to 3.0 ft) tall at the shoulder. Males can weigh up to 160 kg (350 lb).[36] females (generally 10–20% smaller than males) can weigh as little as 70 kg (150 lb), but can also weigh up to 125 kg (276 lb). Average adult weight is 100 to 115 kg (220 to 254 lb).

  • Distribution:

    The giant panda lives in a few mountain ranges in central China, mainly in Sichuan, but also in neighbouring Shaanxi and Gansu.

  • Conservation status:

  • Subspecies:

    • Ailuropoda melanoleuca melanoleuca
    • Ailuropoda melanoleuca qinlingensis- Qinling panda
